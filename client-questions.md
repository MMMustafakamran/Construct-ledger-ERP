# Client Discovery Questions — ERP Accounting Software

> Keep it conversational. These 15 questions are designed to make the client **tell the story** of their problem and what they actually need.

---

## The Problem

**1. Walk me through a normal day — what does your accounting/finance work look like from morning to evening?**
*(This reveals the actual workflow, who does what, and where the pain is.)*

**2. What's the most frustrating part of that process? Where do things go wrong or take too long?**
*(This tells you the #1 problem to solve.)*

**3. Can you show me the Excel sheets and ledger books you currently use?**
*(Seeing the actual data they track is worth more than 50 questions. Ask them to share samples.)*

---

## The Scope

**4. You mentioned equipment, job orders, vendors, customers — which of these is the MOST important to get right first? If you could only fix ONE thing, what would it be?**
*(Forces prioritization. You can't build everything at once.)*

**5. How many people will use this, and what do they each need to do?**
*(e.g., "Just me and my accountant" vs. "10 people across 3 sites" — completely changes the design.)*

**6. When you say "job order," what exactly does that mean in your business? Can you give me a real example from start to finish?**
*(This is the core of construction ERP — make sure you understand it concretely, not abstractly.)*

---

## The Money Flow

**7. Walk me through what happens when you BUY something — from deciding you need it, to paying the vendor.**
*(Reveals the Procure-to-Pay process — POs, approvals, invoices, payments.)*

**8. Walk me through what happens when you BILL a customer — from the work being done to getting paid.**
*(Reveals the Quote-to-Cash process — invoicing, AR, collections.)*

**9. How do you track which costs belong to which job or equipment? Can you show me an example?**
*(This is the #1 differentiator of this ERP — cost allocation to jobs/equipment.)*

---

## The Equipment Question

**10. For your equipment — what information do you absolutely NEED to track, and what would be NICE to track?**
*(The handwritten notes list GPS, permits, component depreciation — find out which are must-haves vs. wishes.)*

**11. Do you need to know "how much money did this specific excavator make or cost me this year"? How do you figure that out today?**
*(Validates the equipment-level P&L requirement and shows how painful it is today.)*

---

## The Practical Stuff

**12. Who will use this — tech-savvy people with accounting knowledge, or field workers who barely use computers?**
*(Determines UI complexity. "My accountant and site supervisors" = very different users.)*

**13. Does this need to work on phones at job sites, or only on office computers?**
*(Web vs. desktop vs. mobile — changes everything.)*

**14. Do you have any deadline or event driving this? Is there a financial year-end, audit, or growth milestone coming up?**
*(Tells you the timeline and urgency.)*

**15. Is there any ERP or accounting software you've seen and liked, even if it didn't fully fit? What did you like about it?**
*(Gives you a design reference point and reveals unspoken expectations.)*

---

## 💡 Tip for the conversation

Don't read these questions like a checklist. Start with **Q1 and Q2** and just **listen**. Most of the other answers will come out naturally as the client talks about their problems. Use the remaining questions to fill gaps.

The goal is to leave the meeting being able to say:
> *"They run a construction business with X equipment and Y active jobs. Today they track everything in Excel/books and the biggest pain is ___. They need a system that lets them ___, ___, and ___, used by ___ people on ___."*

If you can fill that sentence, you understand the project.
